# TAD premium service delivery system

## Purpose

Make the existing six paid services feel complete, controlled and worth their current prices without adding feature categories, increasing operator workload or changing the system boundary.

TAD sells organised admin outcomes. The workspace, queue, approvals and reports are evidence of the service—not a substitute for the service.

## Non-negotiable operating rule

Every active record must have:

- one visible status;
- one responsible owner;
- one next action;
- one due date;
- one blocked reason when work cannot move;
- one recorded outcome when an action is completed.

No operator should create a parallel spreadsheet, private checklist or duplicate report when the same fact already exists in the managed workspace.

## 14-day stabilisation cadence

### Day 0 — Controlled launch

- confirm scope and payment evidence;

- confirm status, owner, next action and due date for every open record;
- record the baseline;

### Day 3 — Data-quality review

- correct unclear or duplicate records;

### Day 7 — First evidence report

- work received;

### Day 14 — Stabilisation decision

- movement against baseline;

- actual operator effort;
- client decisions still outstanding;
- recommendation to Continue, Change or Stop.

## Standard weekly report

Every report uses the same structure:

1. **Scope:** department, active-record boundary and reporting period.
2. **Received:** records entering the workflow.
3. **Moved:** records whose status or next action changed.
4. **Completed:** records reaching a valid closed state.
5. **Open:** current controlled workload.
6. **Overdue:** actions or records beyond the agreed date.
7. **Blocked:** reason, owner and required resolution.
8. **Client decisions:** approvals or answers waiting for authority.
9. **Capacity:** active volume compared with the contracted boundary.
10. **Next week:** the highest-priority work already present in the queue.

Narrative must be assembled from recorded workspace facts. The operator reviews and corrects it; the operator does not manually reconstruct the week from WhatsApp, memory and private notes.

## Operator efficiency rules

- Work from Admin HQ and the managed workspace only.
- Update the existing record instead of writing a separate progress note elsewhere.
- Use the standard status map; do not invent client-specific synonyms without an approved template change.
- Record an outcome and next date in the same interaction.
- Create an approval only when client authority is genuinely required.
- Use blocked reasons for exceptions; do not hide blocked work in free-text notes.
- Review by due date and priority, not by repeatedly opening every client.
- Generate the weekly report from recorded metrics, then add only material context.
- Escalate volume above scope instead of silently absorbing it.
- Close or hand over stale records instead of carrying an indefinite backlog.

## Scope and margin protection

The active-record limit must appear in:

- qualification notes;
- commercial acceptance;
- setup checklist;
- weekly report;
- Day 14 decision;
- continuation review.

When volume exceeds the contracted boundary, the operator records it as a scope decision. The operator must not create hidden unpaid capacity by continuing indefinitely without a commercial decision.

## Client-control protections

The following are positive service boundaries:

- important decisions remain client-approved;
- TAD does not hold banking credentials;
- TAD does not make payments or alter bank details;
- external messages are not sent automatically;
- legal, tax, accounting and clinical decisions remain outside scope;
- reports prove recorded control and activity, not guaranteed revenue or business outcomes.

## Quality acceptance

A service is premium when the client can answer, without asking the operator:

- What is in scope?
- What moved this week?
- What is overdue?
- What is blocked?
- What needs my decision?
- Are we above the agreed record limit?
- What happens next?

If the portal and report cannot answer those questions from existing records, the fix is data quality, wording or operating discipline—not a new feature.

## Change rule

- promise autonomous messaging;
- increase weekly deliverables;
- introduce unlimited records;
- require a duplicate operator tracker;
- change current prices;
- weaken human approval;
- blur TAD Managed with DueToday or TAD SaaS.
