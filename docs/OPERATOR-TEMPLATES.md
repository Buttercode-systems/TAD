# TAD operator templates

Use these templates for every department. Replace bracketed fields only. Do not create parallel formats for individual clients unless the operating contract changes.

## 1. Qualification checklist

- Business: [name]
- Department: [department]
- Main repeated failure: [problem]
- Active record count: [count]
- Published record boundary: [limit]
- Current tools: [tools]
- Decision-maker available: Yes / No
- Data authority confirmed: Yes / No
- Human-approval boundary accepted: Yes / No
- Fit decision: Qualified / Needs separate scope / Declined
- Reason: [one sentence]

## 2. Commercial acceptance and payment proof

- Published setup price: [amount]
- Published monthly price: [amount]
- One-workflow scope accepted: Yes / No
- Active-record boundary accepted: Yes / No
- Start date accepted: [date]
- Primary client email confirmed: [email]
- Setup payment status: Received / Approved exception / Not received
- Evidence reference: [reference]
- Workspace may be created: Yes / No

## 3. Record-transfer instruction

Send only records inside the agreed [department] workflow and active-record boundary of [limit]. Use the supplied template. Do not send passwords, banking credentials, unnecessary identity documents, clinical information or records outside the agreed scope. Each row must identify the current status, owner where known, next action where known and due date where known.

## 4. Import and cleanup checklist

- Count source rows.
- Remove exact duplicates.
- Flag possible duplicates instead of guessing.
- Confirm each record belongs to the agreed workflow.
- Normalize only approved statuses.
- Preserve source references.
- Add missing owner as Unassigned, not a guessed person.
- Add missing next action as Needs next action.
- Add missing due date as Not set and surface it for review.
- Record blocked reasons separately from general notes.
- Compare final loaded count with the contracted boundary.
- Record excluded rows and reason.

## 5. Day 0 launch checklist

- Scope and payment proof confirmed.
- Correct department template installed.
- Starting records loaded and counted.
- Every open record has status, owner, next action and due date or an explicit missing-data state.
- Approval and escalation boundary confirmed.
- Client manager access verified.
- Viewer restrictions verified when applicable.
- Baseline recorded.
- Day 3, Day 7 and Day 14 dates scheduled.

## 6. Weekly report review

- Reporting period correct.
- Received, moved, completed, open, overdue and blocked totals reconcile with workspace records.
- Pending client decisions listed.
- Active volume compared with scope.
- Material data-quality issues stated.
- Next-week priorities already exist in the queue.
- No guaranteed outcome language.
- Client response requested: Continue / Change / Stop.

## 7. Approval escalation

**Decision required:** [clear decision]

**Why it is needed:** [business reason]

**Record(s) affected:** [reference]

**Deadline:** [date]

**Options:** [approve / reject or defined choices]

**What TAD will do after the decision:** [next action]

No payment, bank-detail change, legal commitment or external promise may be inferred from a general approval.

## 8. Pause, close or exit checklist

- Stop creating new operational work.
- Record final service status.
- Resolve, close or explicitly hand over every open record.
- Record pending client decisions.
- Issue final report.
- Confirm data retention decision.
- Remove access that is no longer required.
- Preserve required audit history.
- Record final active volume and operator effort.
